## Structure and Tone

- ﻿﻿The poem is written in two stanzas of rhymed couplets (AABBCCDD...), creating a simple, musical quality that represents a childlike voice.
- ﻿﻿The repetition of lines ("Little Lamb, who made thee?") and children song rhythm reinforce innocence and simplicity.

## Themes

1. Innocence and Creation
- ﻿﻿The lamb symbolizes purity, and vulnerability. It is both an actual lamb and a Christian symbol of Jesus Christ ('He is called by thy name / For He calls Himself a Lamb").
- ﻿﻿The act of creation is presented as gentle and loving-God is a parental figure, giving the lamb  
    "clothing of delight" (wool) and a "tender voice."

2. Christ and Redemption
- ﻿﻿The lamb is directly linked to Christ as "the Lamb of God," highlighting themes of sacrifice, divine love, and salvation.
- ﻿﻿The childlike speaker shows unquestioning faith in the goodness of creation.

2. Innocent Perspective
- The poem reflects the worldview of innocence: the universe is harmonious, God is generous, and creation is tenderly ordered.
