#### Favourite character
My preferred character in "Io e te" is Lorenzo Cuni, the central protagonist. What captivates me about him is his profound character development throughout the story. I admire how he initially strives to conform and blend in with others to fit societal norms. However, as the narrative unfolds, he begins to realize the emptiness of conformity. It's during the week he spends in the cellar that he comes to the touching realization that he would have rather joined his peers in Cortina, embracing his true self, rather than hiding it.

#### Despised character
Olivia, Lorenzo's stepsister, stands out as the character I found most disliked in the story. She seemed to create the most obstacles for Lorenzo during his time in the cellar. But its also true that she helps him finding himself.
#### Favourite moment
One of my favorite moments in the book occurs when Lorenzo, in his quest for sleeping pills for her stepsister, ventures into his dying grandmother's hospital room. While there, he narrates a  story to his grandmother, providing solace not only to her but also to himself. This is because i really like how they behave towards each other.

#### Would you recommend this book?
Absolutely, I would recommend "Io e te." It's an accessible and engaging read that doesn't demand a significant time commitment. I've personally found it so compelling that I've read it three times already.

#### Key lesson from the book
The central lesson imparted by the book is the importance of authenticity. It underscores that true happiness can only be achieved by embracing and being true to oneself. Additionally, the narrative carries a secondary lesson against the use of drugs, emphasizing their negative consequences.
