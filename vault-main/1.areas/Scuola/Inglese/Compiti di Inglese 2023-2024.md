## Lavoro svolto
1. Funzioni comunicative: parlare del futuro, obbligo, leggi e regole, parlare di azioni in corso nel passato, abilità nel passato, parlare di azioni iniziate nel passato e tuttora in corso, abitudini nel passato, essere abituato/abituarsi a fare qualcosa; abilità o specifiche situazioni nel passato; chiedere e dare consigli, immaginare situazioni ipotetiche, esprimere desideri e rimpianti; parlare di obblighi e permessi; esprimere deduzioni al presente o al passato; riportare affermazioni, domande, ordini, suggerimenti, consigli etc; aree lessicali: consumerism and the environment, environmental issues; memories, describing objects; popular trends/fads, describing clothes; choices and decisions, age and the law; money and marketing, shops and shopping; feelings and emotions, music/mood/health and fitness; personalities: metaphors, personality adjectives and qualities; be yourself; summer activities, summer camps; crimes and criminals, reporting verbs.
2. Strutture grammaticali: future forms: be going to/will/present continuous/might; pronomi relativi (defining e non-defining relative clauses), quantifiers; reflexive pronouns and each other; comparative and superlative adverbs; past tenses/ past narrative tenses: present perfect vs simple past, present perfect simple and continuous, duration form – for and since, present perfect with just/already/yet/not yet/ still, simple past and past continuous, past perfect; vari usi di will e shall; zero, first, second, third conditionals, unless; time clauses; reported speech: reported statements; future in the past; reporting verbs and verb patterns; modal verbs: mustn’t vs don’t have to, may, might, could, shall, should/ ought to/ had better; can, could, manage to, be able to; should have done/ ought to have done; modal verbs for speculation in the past and in the present; would rather; used to, be/get used to; tre usi e strutture di wish: wish/if only + simple past / would + base form/ had + p.p.; make/let/allowed to; collocations: make and do; LINKERS (contrast, concession, addition, cause and result, sequence, to make a list, to introduce a topic, to conclude a topic); ing forms and infinitives; PHRASAL VERBS.
3. LIFE SKILLS: Unit 3 Dealing with a moral dilemma p.29; Unit 5 Managing emotions p. 47.
PREPARAZIONE ALLA CERTIFICAZIONE Cambridge English PRELIMINARY (PET):
➢ esercitazioni complete di PET READING, LISTENING, SPEAKING
➢ PET WRITING: writing an E-MAIL, a STORY, an ARTICLE (materiale inviato su Classroom)

LEARNING TO LEARN : improving reading skills; improving listening skills; making mind maps; phonetics and pronunciation, intonation; how to use the online monolingual dictionary.
SELF-ASSESSMENT: schede di autovalutazione dopo la verifica scritta.
