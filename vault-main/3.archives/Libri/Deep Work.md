**Deep Work**: Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. These efforts create new value, improve your skill, and are hard to replicate.

**Shallow Work**: Noncognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks, often performed while distracted. These efforts tend to not create much new value in the world and are easy to replicate.

## Rule 4 Drain the Shallows
- treat shallow work with suspicion
- Schedule Every Minute of Your Day(you can batch similar things into more generic task blocks)
	1. Problem:  your estimates will prove wrong
	2. Problem: you’ll be interrupted and new obligations will unexpectedly appear on your plate. These events will also break your schedule(when this happens at the next available moment, take a few minutes to create a revised schedule for the time that remains in the day.)
	3. Problem: you’re going to underestimate at first how much time you require for most things(you should make an effort to accurately (if not somewhat conservatively) predict the time tasks will require.)
	- use of overflow conditional blocks(block off the expected time, then follow this with an additional block, use this additional block to keep working on it)
- Quantify the Depth of Every Activity
- Ask your boss for a **Shallow work budget**
